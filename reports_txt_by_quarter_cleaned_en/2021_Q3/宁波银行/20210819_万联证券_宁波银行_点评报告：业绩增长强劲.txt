On August 14, 2021, the Bank published its semi-annual report for 2021.
Prior to the provision, profits increased by 24.7 per cent over the same period, while net profits attributable to the parent increased by 21.4 per cent over the same year: at the end of the income, net income from interest increased by 21.91 per cent over the same year and net income from fees decreased by 0.68 per cent. At the end of the provision, the provision for non-credit assets increased further.
At the end of the interest-bearing liability, the cost of deposits rose. In terms of the increase in deposits, the company’s clients grew more than at the beginning of the year.
This represents an increase of $75.5 billion compared to the beginning of the year, with savings deposits of $22.4 billion, an increase of $23.2 billion compared to the beginning of the year; Fund holdings of $10.9 billion, an increase of $24.1 billion compared to the beginning of the year.
This represents an increase of 3,592 households from the beginning of the year; private bank clients total $128.8 billion, an increase of $33.2 billion from the beginning of the year, with an average household asset of $11.4 million.
Profit forecasting and investment proposals: The company portfolio programme was approved in July, and future capital replenishment will help to further expand the size of the company. The wealth management business is also advancing steadily.
