Russia prohibits the export of diesel fuel, and the profits from the domestic export of finished oil are expected to rise.
On the Russian government’s website, on 21 September, news was released that the Russian government had temporarily restricted the export of gasoline and diesel, with the aim of stabilizing the price of oil in the Russian domestic market. In the first 20 days of September, Russia reduced its exports of diesel and gasoline by sea by nearly 30% from 1.7 million tons in the same period in August, as local refineries entered seasonal repairs and faced fuel shortages and price increases in the domestic market.
