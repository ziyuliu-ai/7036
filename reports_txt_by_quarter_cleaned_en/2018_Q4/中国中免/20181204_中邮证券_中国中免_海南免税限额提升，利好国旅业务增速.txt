China Travel (60188.sh): The Hainan tax-exempt limit has been raised and the Litteng operation has increased rapidly.
The policy bulletin regulates part of the policy on duty-free shopping for passengers from Hainan Island. First, to increase the cumulative tax-free shopping limit per person per year for passengers from the island, including residents of the island, to $30,000 per person per year. Second, to increase some items of household medical equipment, which are in the process of separation.
The island’s list of duty-free goods includes visual training devices, hearing aids, orthopaedic fixtures, and domestic respiratory support devices (non-life support), which are limited to two units per person.
Tourists will also benefit from tax-free shopping policies, and two additional duty-free shops will be created in Hainan.
The further liberalization of the Sea Island tax-exempt policy will continue to promote the development of the tax-free marketing industry. This upgrade of the Treasury Department’s duty-free exit line is a deepening of our foreign import opening and development, and a reaffirmation of Hainan as a pilot reform pilot.
