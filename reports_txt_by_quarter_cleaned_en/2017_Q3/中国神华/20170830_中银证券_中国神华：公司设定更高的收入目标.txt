Liu Zhisheng, net profit under the inter-cfa accounting standards is $26.3 billion, an increase of 143 per cent over the year, which is consistent with the previous guidelines.
In the second quarter of 2017, the net profit ring grew by 3%. China’s net profit under accounting standards grew by 147% in the same year, with a strong increase in revenue derived mainly from coal operations. The profits from coal operations grew by 373% in the same year, with the main increase in home-grown coal sales by 18% in the same year, as a result of an average increase of 57% in coal prices.
The system worked well, with the cost of producing own coal units falling by 4 per cent on a year-on-year basis under IAS. Thus, the profitability of the coal industry increased from 9.7 per cent in the second half of 2016 to 25.4 per cent in the first half of 2017.
The worst performance in the first half of 2017 was in the power generation business. Business profits fell by 60% in the same year, owing to higher coal prices.
