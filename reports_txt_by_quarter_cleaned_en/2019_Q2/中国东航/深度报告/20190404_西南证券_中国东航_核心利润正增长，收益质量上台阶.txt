China’s Eastern Airlines Group Ltd. is one of the three main State-owned air transport complexes, dating back to January 1957, when the first flight squadron was set up by the former Civil Aviation Administration in Shanghai. In 1993, China’s Northwest Airlines, United China Yunnan Airlines, and East China Airlines were joined on the basis of the former company of the Eastern Air Group.
In 1997, East China became the first Chinese airline company to be listed in three locations at the New Exchange, the Union, and the Hong Kong Shipping Company.
