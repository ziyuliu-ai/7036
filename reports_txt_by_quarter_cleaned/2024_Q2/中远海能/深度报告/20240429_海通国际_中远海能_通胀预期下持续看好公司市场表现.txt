Summary
In Q1 2024, the net profit attributable to the owners of the parent company was ¥1.236 billion, a year-on-year increase of 12.76%;
operating income was ¥5.838 billion, a year-on-year increase of 3.68%; basic earnings per share were ¥0.259, a year-on-year
increase of 12.76%.
The performance of crude oil freight rates at the beginning of the year exceeded expectations, reflecting tight supply and demand.
From January to March 2024, the average daily earnings of VLCC-TD3C were $44,000. In March, benefiting from concentrated
cargo releases and tight ship availability in the Middle East region, freight rates temporarily exceeded $70,000.
The prosperity of oil shipping continues, with the potential for a dual increase in freight rates and valuation. On the supply side,
the logic is solid, with VLCC prosperity expected to continue for at least 27 years, sustaining high valuations. Factors that dragged
down oil shipping performance in 2023 on the demand side are expected to reverse.
Investment recommendation: Considering the continued prosperity of VLCCs and the potential for a dual increase in freight rates
and valuation, we continue to recommend COSCO SHIPPING Energy Transportation as the stock with the highest proportion of
billion/¥8.949 billion, corresponding to P/E ratios of 10.19x/9.24x/8.40x. Our target price is unchanged at Rmb20.95 based on
13.6x PER of 2024, and we maintain the 'Outperform' rating.
Risks: OPEC+ cuts exceed expectations, global economic downturn.
