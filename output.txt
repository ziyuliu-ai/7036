Market Trends
The company maintains a leading position in the power battery market and its profitability remains stable. In 2024, the company's power battery sales reached 
381 GWh, a year- on-year increase of 19%, maintaining its position as the world's largest power battery manufacturer for eight consecutive years. The company's gross 
profit margin for power batteries in 2024 was 23.94%, a year- on-year increase of 5.81 percentage points; the unit gross profit was RMB 0.16/ Wh, remaining relatively 
stable year- on-year. The company continues to upgrade its power battery products, launching products such as the Kirin Battery, Shenxing Battery, Xiaoyao Battery, and 
Tianxing Battery to meet the needs of customers in different scenarios for passenger and commercial vehicles.
The company's energy storage batteries are highly profitable, and it is rapidly expanding its overseas customer base. In 2024, the company sold 94 GWh of energy 
storage batteries, a year- on-year increase of 36%, ranking first globally for the fourth consecutive year. The gross margin of the company's energy storage battery 
systems was 26.84% in 2024, a year- on-year increase of 8.19%; the unit gross profit was RMB 0.16/ Wh, a slight year- on-year increase. The company continued to make new 
progress with overseas customers. Since 2025, the company has successively won large orders such as the UAE RTC 19 GWh energy storage project and the uinbrook

In 2024, the company achieved revenue of RMB 362.013 billion, a year- on-year decrease of 10%; net profit attributable to the parent company was RMB 50.745 billion, a year- on-year increase of 
15%; net profit attributable to the parent company after deducting non- recurring items was RMB 44.993 billion, a year- on-year increase of 12%; gross profit margin under the new accounting 
standards was 24.44%, a year- on-year increase of 5.25 percentage points; net profit margin was 14.92%, a year- on-year increase of 3.26 percentage points.
In Q4 2024, the company achieved revenue of RMB 102.968 billion, a year- on-year decrease of 3% and a quarter- on-quarter increase of 12%; net profit attributable to the parent company was RMB 
14.744 billion, a year- on-year increase of 14% and a quarter- on-quarter increase of 12%; and net profit attributable to the parent company excluding non- recurring items was RMB 12.817 billion, a year- on-year decrease of 2% and a quarter- on-quarter increase of 12%.
+6%. The company's apparent gross profit margin in Q4 2024 was 15.04%, a year- on-year decrease of 10.62 percentage points and a quarter- on-quarter decrease of 16.13 percentage points; this 
was mainly due to the retrospective adjustment of accounting standards in the annual report and the relocation of after- sales service fees from sales expenses to operating costs. Under the old 
accounting standards, the company's gross profit margin in Q4 2024 would have been 26.54%, a year- on-year increase of 0.88 percentage points and a quarter- on-quarter decrease of 6%.
- 4.63 percentage points. The company's net profit margin in Q4 2024 was 14.83%, up 1.43 percentage points year- on-year and down 0.18 percentage points quarter- on-quarter.

The company is accelerating its capacity expansion and its global footprint is enhancing its service capabilities. The company's battery production in the second half of 
2024 was 305 GWh, with an estimated capacity utilization rate exceeding 90%. Against the backdrop of continued strong end- user demand, the company accelerated its capacity 
expansion. As of the end of 2024, the company's existing capacity was 676 GWh, with 219 GWh under construction, for a total planned capacity (existing capacity + capacity under 
construction) of 895 GWh, an increase of 96 GWh compared to the end of the first half of 2024.
The company is accelerating its global expansion, with its German production capacity continuing to ramp up and improve efficiency. The Hungarian factory, the Spanish joint 
venture factory, and the Indonesian battery supply chain project are all under active construction or in the planning stages. Furthermore, according to Co-Chairman Pan Jian, 
the company expects to finalize its fourth battery factory in Europe by 2025. The company is steadily advancing its battery production capacity construction to meet the 
delivery needs of global customer orders.

The company's power battery products are continuously iterated and upgraded to serve customers with higher quality and higher performance products. In the 
passenger vehicle sector, the company launched the Kirin Battery and Shenxing Battery to meet the needs of pure electric vehicle customers for charging speed, driving range, 
and power. Addressing the issue of short pure electric range in hybrid vehicles, the company released the Xiaoyao Supercharged Hybrid Battery in October 2024, boasting a pure 
electric range of over 400km and 4C supercharging capabilities. In the commercial vehicle sector, the company launched the Tianxing Battery series, precisely tailored to 
scenarios such as buses, logistics vehicles, and heavy trucks, effectively solving industry pain points such as short driving range, slow refueling, and rapid lifespan degradation in 
commercial vehicles.
The company continues to secure large overseas energy storage contracts, while other businesses are developing steadily.
The company's market share in energy storage batteries remains stable, and its profitability is steadily improving. In 2024, the company's revenue from energy storage 
battery systems was RMB 57.29 billion, a year- on-year decrease of 4%; gross profit margin was 26.84%, a year- on-year increase of 8.19%. The company's energy storage 
battery sales volume in 2024 was 94 GWh, a year- on-year increase of 36%; the unit selling price was RMB 0.61/ Wh, a year- on-year decrease of RMB 0.26/ Wh; and the unit gross 
profit was RMB 0.16/ Wh, a slight increase compared to the previous quarter. According to SNE Research data, the company's global market share in energy storage batteries 
reached 36.5% in 2024, ranking first globally for four consecutive years.

UAE. In March, Quinbrook planned to partner with CATL to launch a 3GW/ 24GWh energy storage project cluster in Australia.
